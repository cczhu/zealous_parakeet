Another factor to consider is the stability of mass transfer between the binary constituents - if mass transfer is stable, then no merger will occur.  If we estimate the WD equation of state as a polytrope $P = K{\rho}^{\Gamma}$ (where $\Gamma = 5/3$ in the nonrelativistic case, and $\Gamma = 4/3$ in the relativistic case), we obtain a mass-radius relation \citep{camenzindbook}

\begin{equation}
R \propto M^{\frac{\Gamma - 2}{3\Gamma - 4}}.
\label{massradiusrelation}
\end{equation}

In the case of $\Gamma = 5/3$, this gives $R \propto M^{-1/3}$; in the case of $\Gamma = 4/3$, $R$ is independent of $M$.
